Sri Sri Giridhari Dauji Mandir- Manasarovar, Jaipur

Sri Sri Giridhari Dauji Mandir, located in Mansarovar, Jaipur, is a prominent ISKCON temple inaugurated in 2019. Inspired by Srila Prabhupada’s vision, the temple features exquisite pink sandstone architecture and 52 stained-glass windows depicting Krishna’s pastimes. It is dedicated to the beautiful deities of Sri Sri Giridhari Dauji (Krishna and Balaram). The temple serves as a vibrant center for devotion, education, and cultural celebration in Rajasthan.

Temple

History

Sri Sri Giridhari Dauji Mandir in Jaipur was envisioned by Srila Prabhupada in 1975 to establish a Krishna-Balaram temple similar to the one in Vrindavan. Construction began in the 1990s under the guidance of senior ISKCON leaders. The grand pink sandstone temple was officially inaugurated in 2018. It now stands as a major spiritual and cultural landmark in Rajasthan, attracting thousands of devotees.
