Radha Rasabihari Mandir- Juhu, Mumbai

ISKCON Indore, also known as the Sri Sri Radha Govind Temple, is a prominent spiritual center located in Nipania, Indore. Established in 2004, it features beautiful deities of Radha Govind, Jagannath Baladev Subhadra, and Gaura Nitai. The temple is known for its peaceful atmosphere, daily kirtans, and community outreach programs. It also runs a goshala and distributes free prasadam as part of its service mission.

Temple

History

Sri Sri Radha Rasabihari Mandir in Juhu, Mumbai was established by Srila Prabhupada in the early 1970s after much struggle to secure the land. The temple was officially inaugurated on January 14, 1978, and has since grown into one of ISKCON’s most iconic centers. Built with white marble and a grand dome, it houses the beautiful deities of Radha Rasabihari, Gaura Nitai, and Sita-Rama Lakshman Hanuman. Today, it serves as a vibrant spiritual hub attracting thousands of devotees and visitors every year.
