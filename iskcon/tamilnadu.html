Radha Krishna Mandir- Akkarai,Chennai

ISKCON Chennai, also known as Sri Sri Radha Krishna Mandir, was inaugurated on 26 April 2012 and is the largest Radha-Krishna temple in Tamil Nadu, built over 1.5 acres in Akkarai, Sholinganallur . Constructed with teak-wood altars sourced from Jaipur and Odisha, the temple is inspired by Pallava and Kalinga architectural styles, and spreads across five levels including an auditorium and prasadam hall . Designed as part of a Centre for Spiritual Art and Culture, it features a marble cosmic mandala at the entrance, a chandelier of Himalayan quartz crystals, and serene interior spaces for meditation and cultural programs .

History

The establishment of ISKCON Chennai began in 2002 after Srila Prabhupada’s 1971 direction to spread Krishna consciousness in Madras (Chennai) . With donations from over 8,000 life patrons, construction started on 17 March 2002 and culminated in the kumbhabhishekam and deity installation on 26 April 2012, celebrated with flowers dropped from a helicopter . The temple relocated deities from its T. Nagar unit to this prominent location on East Coast Road, establishing it as a cultural and spiritual landmark in southern Chennai .
