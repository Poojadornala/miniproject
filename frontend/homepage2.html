Sanatana Temples

Ancient & Traditional Krishna Temples are sacred sites rooted in timeless devotion and spiritual heritage. They include Swayambhu temples, where Lord Krishna is believed to have manifested naturally, and historic temples established by kings, sages, and local communities over centuries. Untouched by modern institutional influence, these temples preserve the rituals, architecture, and divine atmosphere of India’s rich Vedic tradition. They reflect the pure spiritual essence of Sanatana Dharma, offering devotees a deep, authentic connection to Lord Krishna.

iskcon temples

ISKCON (International Society for Krishna Consciousness) is a global movement founded in 1966 by Srila Prabhupada to spread the teachings of Lord Krishna through bhakti yoga. Rooted in scriptures like the Bhagavad Gita and Srimad Bhagavatam, ISKCON promotes devotional practices such as chanting, kirtans, and deity worship. Its temples are known for their vibrant spiritual atmosphere, cultural programs, and global outreach.

Bagavadgita

The Bhagavad Gita (meaning “Song of the Lord”) is one of the most important spiritual texts in Hindu philosophy. It is a part of the Indian epic Mahabharata, and takes the form of a dialogue between Lord Krishna and the warrior Arjuna, taking place on the battlefield of Kurukshetra.
