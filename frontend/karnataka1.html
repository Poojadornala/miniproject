Udupi Sri Krishna Matha Temple

Udupi Shri Krishna Temple is a historic Hindu temple dedicated to Krishna, located in Udupi, Karnataka. The temple area is like a living Aashram, surrounded by other ancient temples.

Temple

History

The Krishna Matha was founded by Madhvacharya in the 13th century, who established the Dvaita school of Vedanta. The deity is worshipped through the Navagraha Kindi window. The temple feeds thousands daily, earning the name Anna Brahma.

Seva Offers

The Ashta Mathas manage the daily sevas and temple administration in rotation. Each Matha has its own deity. Udupi is also known for Dvaita philosophy and Daasa Saahitya.

Bhagavad Gita Slokam

Chapter 13, Verse 14

सर्वतः पाणिपादं तत्सर्वतोऽक्षिशिरोमुखम् |
सर्वतः श्रुतिमल्लोके सर्वमावृत्य तिष्ठति || 14||
sarvataḥ pāṇi-pādaṁ tat sarvato ’kṣhi-śhiro-mukham
sarvataḥ śhrutimal loke sarvam āvṛitya tiṣhṭhati

Translation

Everywhere are His hands and feet, eyes, heads, and faces. His ears too are in all places, for He pervades everything in the universe.

Extra Information

Udupi Sri Krishna Matha Temple is famous for its unique style of worship and management by eight monasteries. It attracts devotees from across the world.
