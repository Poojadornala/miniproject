Guruvayur Temple

The Guruvayur Temple is dedicated to Guruvayurappan, a form of Vishnu (Krishna). It is located in Guruvayur, Kerala, and is considered one of the most sacred pilgrimage centers in South India.

Temple Image

History

Historical references to Guruvayur date back to the 14th century. The temple is also mentioned in Tamil literature and gained popularity by the 16th century as a major pilgrimage destination.

Bhagavad Gita Slokam

Chapter 10, Verse 23

रुद्राणां शङ्करश्चास्मि वित्तेशो यक्षरक्षसाम् ||
वसूनां पावकश्चास्मि मेरुः शिखरिणामहम् || 23||

rudrāṇāṁ śhaṅkaraśh chāsmi vitteśho yakṣha-rakṣhasām
vasūnāṁ pāvakaśh chāsmi meruḥ śhikhariṇām aham

Translation

Among the Rudras I am Shankar, among Yakshas and Rakshasas I am Kubera. Among the Vasus I am Agni, and among mountains I am Meru.
