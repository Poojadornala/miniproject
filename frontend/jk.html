Radha Krishan Mandir In Jammu-and-Kashmir is a treasured place for the Hindu community in the region and holds a deep spiritual connectedness to Hindu culture and tradition. Worship method

Radha Krishan Mandir in Jammu-and-Kashmir follows the Hindu temple worship method, which includes prayer, puja, aarti, sankalp, and bhajan. The temple is based on the teachings of Radha Krishan. The devotees visit this temple throughout the year to offer prayers, take part in different rituals, seek blessings. The traditional Hindu style of temple worship in Radha Krishan Mandir includes numerous activities like chanting mantras, reciting specific prayers, offering flowers and incense in front of the deity, singing praises and bhajans dedicated to the deity, and aarti. All these activities leading up to aarti is done with utmost reverence and devotion to Radha Krishan. Along with these traditional methods of temple worship there are other activities like deep meditation and contemplation to receive spiritual knowledge about the Divine. Bhagavad Gita Slokam

Chapter 10, Verse 41

यद्यद्विभूतिमत्सत्त्वं श्रीमदूर्जितमेव वा |
तत्देवावगच्छ त्वं मम तेजोंऽशसम्भवम् || 41||
yad yad vibhūtimat sattvaṁ śhrīmad ūrjitam eva vā
tat tad evāvagachchha tvaṁ mama tejo ’nśha-sambhavam

Translation

Whatever you see as beautiful, glorious, or powerful, know it to spring from but a spark of My splendor.
