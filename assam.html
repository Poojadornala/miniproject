HARE KRISHNA TEMPLE

The Hare Krishna Temple in North Guwahati,assam is a branch of the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON), which is a worldwide organization dedicated to the practice and promotion of the Hare Krishna movement, also known as the Gaudiya Vaishnavism tradition.

Temple

History

The temple in North Guwahati was established to provide a place for devotees to gather, worship, and practice their faith. The temple hosts regular bhajans (devotional singing), kirtans (musical chanting), and arati (worship ceremonies) throughout the day. In addition to the daily worship activities, the temple also offers a variety of services and programs, such as spiritual guidance, counseling, and educational workshops on Vedic philosophy and culture. The temple also provides prasad (sanctified food) to visitors and hosts festivals and celebrations throughout the year, including Janmashtami, Radha Ashtami, and Holi.Overall, the Hare Krishna Temple in North Guwahati serves as a spiritual center for devotees and seekers alike, offering opportunities for worship, learning, and community building in the context of the Hare Krishna tradition.

Bhagavad Gita Slokam

Chapter 10, Verse 8

अहं सर्वस्य प्रभवो मत्त: सर्वं प्रवर्तते |
इति मत्वा भजन्ते मां बुधा भावसमन्विता: || 8||

ahaṁ sarvasya prabhavo mattaḥ sarvaṁ pravartate
iti matvā bhajante māṁ budhā bhāva-samanvitāḥ

Translation

I am the origin of all creation. Everything proceeds from Me. The wise who know this perfectly worship Me with great faith and devotion.
